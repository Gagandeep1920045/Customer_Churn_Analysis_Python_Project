# Customer_Churn_Analysis_Python_Project
## 1. Project Title/ Headline 📑
### Telecom Customer Churn Analysis📉
Predicting and Visualizing Customer Retention Patterns to Minimize Revenue Loss
## 2. Short Description / Purpose 🎯
This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a telecommunications dataset to identify the key factors driving customer churn. By analyzing customer demographics, account characteristics, and service usage, the project provides actionable insights to help telecommunications providers improve customer retention strategies and optimize business performance.
## 3. Tech Stack💻
* Language: Python
* Data Manipulation: Pandas, NumPy
* Data Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn
* Software: Jupyter Notebook
## 4.Features/Highlights 💡
### 📌 Business Problem
Customer churn is the rate at which the customers stop doing business with an Organisation and it is a critical metric in the telecom industry. High churn rates lead to significant revenue loss and increased customer acquisition costs. The business needs to understand why its customers are leaving to address these issues and retain their subscriber base the analysis is conducted.
### 📌 Goal of the Analysis
* To identify the primary drivers of churn among telecom subscribers.
* To segment the customers based on their churn risk through demographic and account analysis.
* To provide data-driven recommendations for reducing churn and increasing customer lifetime value.
### 📌 Walk Through of key Visuals
#### Bar Charts and Line Graphs:
* The visual representation of churn by payment method clearly shows that
customers using electronic checks churn almost three times as much as those
using more traditional or secure methods like credit cards.
* Customer tenure vs churn rate visualizations reveal a clear declining trend in
churn as customers tenure increases, underscoring the need for early-stage
customer loyalty programs
#### Percentage Distribution of Churn Across Factors:
* Payment Methods: 45% churn for electronic check users, 15% for credit card
users.
* Contract Types: 42% churn for month-to-month contracts, 11% for yearly
contracts, 3% for two-year contracts.
* Tenure: 50% churn in the first year, dropping to 15% after three years.
### 📌 Business Impact & Insights
* Contract Type: Customers on month-to-month contracts show a higher tendency
to churn compared to those on yearly  
contracts. This suggests that
long-term contracts may improve customer retention.
* Payment Methods: A significant proportion of customers using electronic checks
are more likely to churn compared to those using other payment methods (credit
cards, bank transfers, etc).
* The analysis reveals that senior citizens (aged 65+) have a churn rate of 41%,
compared to a 26% churn rate among non-senior citizens.
